Investment Thesis
4/92–96 Albion Street represents a rare investment opportunity in one of Sydney's most sought-after inner-city suburbs. The Cotality AVM at $1.53M is almost certainly understated—it fails to capture the significant premium that converted heritage warehouse residences command over generic apartment stock.

The strongest comparable is Unit 3 in the identical building, which sold for $1.9M in September 2025 after just 11 days on market. This exceptional 11 DOM (vs 37 suburb median) and premium price reflect strong buyer demand for warehouse character. Unit 4 is 12m² smaller (100m² vs 112m²) but offers identical heritage architecture, tri-level layout, and double-height ceilings.

The property's 1925 provenance as the St Francis School Hall conversion, tri-level design, stained oak floorboards, and integrated home office align perfectly with post-COVID flexible work preferences. The courtyard adds rare private outdoor space for an inner-city warehouse apartment.

Capital growth metrics are compelling: $586K (Jul 2002) → $1.78M (Oct 2022) = 20-year CAGR of 5.7%, with recent acceleration to 8.7% p.a. (Oct 2022–Jun 2023). The June 2023 under-contract listing in just 1 DOM (Belle Property) further confirms exceptional buyer interest.

Fair valuation of $1.75M–$1.95M is supported by three independent approaches:
  • Capital Growth: 6–7% forward extrapolation yields $1.75M–$1.95M
  • Income/Yield: $1.2K/week rent at 3.4–3.6% gross yield targets $1.75M–$1.85M
  • Comparable Sales: Unit 3 at $1.9M adjusted for 12m² size difference = $1.77M–$1.81M

For capital-growth-focused investors, this property offers a rare, trophy-quality warehouse conversion with strong market tailwinds (constrained supply, easing rates, rental demand). For owner-occupiers, the flexible work-from-home integration and heritage character justify premium pricing.
