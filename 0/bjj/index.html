Why Private Lessons Make Sense

Given the short two-week window and James being a complete beginner, private lessons offer some real advantages over group classes alone:

Flexibility — Book around holiday plans rather than being locked into a fixed timetable. No rushing to make a 4pm class.

Faster learning — A coach's undivided attention means James picks up fundamentals properly from day one, with real-time corrections rather than copying other kids and hoping for the best.

Confidence — Walking into a group class as the only new person can be daunting at 14. A couple of privates first means he arrives with some knowledge and confidence.

Honest assessment — After a few sessions, the coach can give a genuine view on whether James has an aptitude for it and what to pursue next.

Pricing note: Private BJJ lessons in Sydney typically range from $80–$150 per session (45–60 min). Group classes on casual or short-term passes are usually $25–$50 per session. Neither dojo lists prices online — you’ll need to ask directly.

Our Recommendation

Vantage is the stronger option for this trip. It’s much closer to Elizabeth Bay, explicitly offers private lessons with flexible scheduling, and has a dedicated 10+ kids class that James can drop into. The instructor’s credentials are world-class.

Suggested approach:

  1. Call Adam at Vantage (0435 845 004) and explain the situation — 14-year-old beginner, in Sydney for two weeks, wants to try BJJ.

This hybrid approach gives James the best of both worlds — proper personalised coaching and the fun of rolling with other young people — all within a two-week window.
